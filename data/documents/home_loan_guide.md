# BNB Home Loan Guide

## Who is Eligible

A BNB Home Loan is available to salaried individuals, self-employed professionals, and business owners who meet the following criteria:

- Indian resident, 21 to 65 years of age at the time of loan maturity
- Minimum 2 years of continuous employment (salaried) or 3 years of business continuity (self-employed)
- Credit score of 700 or above (CIBIL or equivalent bureau score)
- The property must be within India and free of legal disputes

Joint applications are permitted. A co-applicant with income increases the eligible loan amount.

## How Much Can You Borrow

BNB uses a Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio (FOIR) of 40-50% when assessing eligibility. This means your total monthly EMI obligations -- including the proposed home loan EMI -- should not exceed 40-50% of your net monthly income. Existing loan EMIs, credit card minimum dues, and other fixed commitments are all counted toward this limit.

The loan amount is also subject to a Loan-to-Value (LTV) ceiling set by RBI based on the property value:
- Properties valued up to Rs. 30 lakhs: maximum LTV of 90%
- Properties valued between Rs. 30 lakhs and Rs. 75 lakhs: maximum LTV of 80%
- Properties valued above Rs. 75 lakhs: maximum LTV of 75%

LTV means BNB can fund at most that percentage of the property's market value. You must arrange the balance as a down payment from your own funds. The final loan amount depends on whichever limit is lower: the FOIR-based income limit or the LTV-based property limit.

BNB's standard home loan eligibility formula is up to 60 times your net monthly income, subject to the FOIR ceiling above. For example, a customer with a net monthly income of Rs. 80,000 may be eligible for up to Rs. 48 lakhs, provided the resulting EMI stays within the FOIR limit and the LTV cap is not exceeded.

## Loan Tenure

Home loans are available for a minimum of 5 years and a maximum of 30 years. Longer tenure means lower EMI but higher total interest paid. Shorter tenure reduces the total interest cost but requires a higher monthly payment. Use the EMI calculator on the BNB website to compare different tenure options before applying.

## Required Documents

**Salaried applicants:**
- PAN card and Aadhaar card
- Last 3 months' salary slips
- Last 6 months' bank statement (salary account)
- Form 16 or last 2 years' ITR
- Appointment letter or employment certificate

**Self-employed applicants:**
- PAN card and Aadhaar card
- Last 2 years' ITR with computation
- Profit and loss statement and balance sheet (CA certified)
- Last 12 months' business bank statement

**Property documents (for all applicants):**
- Sale deed or agreement to sell
- Approved building plan
- NOC from builder or society
- Property tax receipts

## The Application Process

1. Submit a loan application online at bnb.co.in/loans or at any BNB branch.
2. A Relationship Manager will contact you within 2 working days to discuss your requirement.
3. BNB initiates a credit appraisal and property valuation.

**After Sanction**

4. A conditional sanction letter is issued within 10 working days of application approval.
5. After the sanction letter, document verification and legal clearance must be completed. For ready-to-move properties, disbursement to the seller typically happens within 5 to 10 working days of the sanction letter. Under-construction properties are disbursed in stages linked to construction milestones.

Partial disbursement is available for under-construction properties, linked to construction milestones.

## Processing Fee

A processing fee is charged at the time of application. Please ask your Relationship Manager for the current fee applicable to your loan amount. Processing fees are non-refundable once the appraisal begins.

## Prepayment and Foreclosure

Home loans at BNB are linked to the floating rate, which means there is no penalty for partial prepayment or full foreclosure at any time. This is in line with RBI guidelines for floating-rate home loans, which prohibit prepayment penalties from the date of disbursement.

## What Happens If You Miss an EMI

Missing an EMI triggers a reminder from the collections team. Persistent default affects your CIBIL score and may lead to a legal notice. If you are facing a financial hardship, contact your Relationship Manager immediately -- BNB offers a restructuring facility for genuine hardship cases.
